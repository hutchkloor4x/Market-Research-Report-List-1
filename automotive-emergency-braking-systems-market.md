<p><p>The automotive emergency braking systems market comprises two key types: foot brakes and hand brakes. Foot brakes, activated by the driver's foot, serve as the primary braking mechanism, automating emergency responses for quick stops to prevent accidents. Hand brakes, typically used for parking, can also function in emergencies, allowing the driver to exert immediate control if the foot brake fails. Both systems enhance vehicle safety, providing critical support in urgent situations by ensuring reliable stopping power.</p></p>
