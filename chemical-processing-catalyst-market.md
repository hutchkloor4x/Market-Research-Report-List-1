Active catalysts are substances that accelerate chemical reactions without undergoing permanent changes themselves, enhancing efficiency in processes like petroleum refining and chemical synthesis. In contrast, deactivation catalysts refer to catalysts that lose their effectiveness due to factors like thermal degradation or poisoning. Understanding these categories is crucial for optimizing industrial processes and maintaining productivity in chemical manufacturing.</p></p>
