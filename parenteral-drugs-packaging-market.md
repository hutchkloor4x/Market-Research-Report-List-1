<p><p>The parenteral drugs packaging market encompasses various materials, with Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) and Polyolefin as prominent types. PVC is widely used for its flexibility and barrier properties, making it suitable for various drug formulations. However, it may pose leaching risks for some medications. In contrast, Polyolefin offers a safer alternative, exhibiting lower risk of chemical interaction and better compatibility with sensitive drugs. Both materials are key in ensuring the integrity and efficacy of parenteral pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
