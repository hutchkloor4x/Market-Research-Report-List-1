<p><p>The sponge rubber market encompasses several types, primarily categorized into closed-cell and open-cell rubber. Closed-cell rubber features a dense structure that offers excellent insulation and water resistance, making it ideal for applications like seals and gaskets. Open-cell rubber, conversely, has a porous structure that allows for air flow and flexibility, often used in cushioning and soundproofing. Additionally, other market segments may include specialized formulations or composites that combine properties for unique applications, broadening the overall market scope.</p></p>
